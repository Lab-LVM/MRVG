Music Reactive Video Generation

Eunjoo Shin, Hankyul Kang, Jongbin Ryu

ICASSP 2025 Submission 396
Code

Schematic overview of our music reactive video generation framework.

Abstract

While generating music-reactive videos is an important task in real-world applications, it has been quite a challenging generation task. We argue that this challenge arises from two main factors: 1) evaluating the music-reactive generation task is fundamentally a subjective matter of individual humans, and thus, 2) applying metrics for quantitative measurement used in existing generative models is tricky. For this reason, in this paper, we introduce an efficient music-reactive video generation model, as well as a metric that can accurately evaluate generated music-reactive videos.For our music-reactive generation model, we present the highlighted chromagram approach to represent musical structure information(e.g., melody, beat, rhythm) in a video effectively. Since musical structure information appears in a very small area in the chromagram, we introduce a highlighting method to augment the structure information in generating videos. We also offer quantitative measurement metric that have a strong correlation with human survey results. In our experiments, we demonstrate that our music-reactive generation model performs favorably, with the proposed metric tending to match the results of human surveys.

Music-Reactive Video Generation Comparison

We generate music-reactive videos on three different music genres (i.e., Rock, K-POP, Traditional Instrumental Ensemble) and compare different generation methods. In every genre, ours work favorably compared to decent baseline methods.
